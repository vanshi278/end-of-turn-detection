Every score is out-of-fold (GroupKFold by turn, 3 seeds) and produced by the official score.py. The same model scores 511 ms in-sample on these folders. That number is memorisation and appears nowhere in our claims.

The result

Why the metric is not what it looks like

The scorer waits delay seconds at each pause and speaks if p_eot ≥ threshold. It sweeps both and reports the lowest mean delay that keeps interrupted turns ≤5%. Two structural facts fall out of that, and they drive every decision we made:

  • A hold pause only costs anything if it outlives the delay. If the user resumes before the agent speaks, nobody notices. Short holds are free.
  • Cost is counted per turn, not per pause. A turn is interrupted if any of its holds fires; the second mistake in a turn is free.

The finding that reorganised the project

Overall AUC averages over every hold, 75% of which the metric does not score. Splitting it out, our model at AUC 0.685 was scoring 0.52 — chance — on the only contrast that pays: turn-end versus a hold long enough to interrupt someone. It had learned to separate turn-ends from short holds, which is worth exactly zero milliseconds. We promoted hard_auc to the headline diagnostic so this could not hide again.

What the model listens to

For each pause, features come only from audio in [0, pause_start). The entry point truncates there and hands the truncated copy to a function that never receives the rest, so feature code cannot see the future rather than merely being careful not to.

Pitch position, not pitch value

The listener's dominant cue was "pitch dropped" → end and "pitch was very high, cant be the end" → hold. Crucially, high and low are relative to that speaker. So we encode the final pitch as a percentile of that speaker's own pitch history, not a z-score: the ear asks "did you come home to your floor?", not "how many IQRs from your median?". It beat the z-score head to head — 0.639 vs 0.595.

Vowel identity without ASR

The listener read Hindi verb endings lexically — "rahega used", "ended with rakhiyega". We cannot run ASR, but Hindi is verb-final and its finite verbs land on a small set of vowel qualities. Cepstral-mean-normalised MFCCs over the final voiced nucleus encode exactly that, and became our strongest Hindi features: mfcc7 = 0.703, mfcc5 = 0.696.

Breath

"person taking breath" → hold. A speaker reloading leaves a trailing unvoiced, noisy, low-energy stretch. unvoiced_frac_final is among our best features in both languages.

Cost-aware training

Holds are weighted by whether they could actually interrupt anyone. A hold shorter than the delay is free and shouldn't consume model capacity. dur is used only at training time, from the labels, exactly like class_weight — it is future information at inference and never reaches a feature.

The honest read on Hindi

Hindi's 850 ms is a degenerate baseline: exactly 5 of its 100 turns contain a hold longer than 0.85 s — exactly the 5% budget — so "fire at everything after 850 ms" saturates the constraint and cannot be beaten without a lower delay. That is a property of this draw of 100 turns, not of Hindi. A single number on a single sample cannot tell "no better than a timer" from "better, but this sample's baseline is degenerate", so we resampled turns.

What we will not claim

Hindi is not yet a demonstrable win. The gain is 46 ms with a 95% interval of [0, 192]. The discrimination is real — hard_auc 0.71 against 0.509 before the listening study — but converting ranking into milliseconds needs more precision on long holds than we have. We would rather say so than quote the 511 ms in-sample number.

Human vs. coding agent

The brief asks who did what. The split below is literal: the human work is work the agent could not do, and it is the reason the Hindi half of this project exists at all.

Human

The listening study — and the features it produced

  • Ran a 36-clip blind listening study (balanced, Hindi-weighted, hearing only what the model hears): 72.2% overall, 75% Hindi, 87.5% on confident Hindi calls. The agent cannot hear audio; this number exists only because a person made it.
  • Established that the Hindi ceiling was not a data ceiling. The agent had proved the model was at chance and could not tell "features are blind" from "signal is absent". 75% blind accuracy on the identical contrast settled it and justified continuing.
  • Native-speaker judgements that became three feature families: pitch-position-within-speaker's-range, breath, and Hindi verb-final vowel quality (→ MFCCs). Each is traceable to a specific written note.
  • Calibrated confidence (conf-5 82%, conf-3 67%), evidence the cue is a real audible property rather than noise.
Coding agent

Infrastructure, DSP, modelling, and the traps

  • Bit-exact reimplementation of the scorer (18 tests) so selection runs on the real metric; hard_auc; the paired bootstrap.
  • Causality by construction + 6-way mutation tests; caught the dataset's file-length leak (AUC 1.000 / 100 ms) and the in-sample trap (511 ms).
  • Vectorised YIN written from scratch (eotlib/yin.py) after finding the voicing gate marked 99% of frames voiced; 0.42% pitch disagreement with pyin at 68× the speed.
  • Cost-aware weighting, rank calibration, the baseline floor (P_FLOOR), model selection, and turning the human's notes into 51 features.

Stated plainly: the agent built the machine and found the traps; the human supplied the perception the machine did not have and the evidence that the remaining gap was worth chasing. Neither half reaches 0.71 Hindi hard_auc alone — the agent's model sat at 0.509 until the listening study, and the study's notes are prose until something turns them into MFCCs and percentile ranks.

Where it fails, and what is next

The residual errors are the ones the human listener also got wrong: syntactically complete clauses where the speaker carried on anyway ("statement looked complete, baat karke gaya tha") — four of six Hindi misses were this. Prosody alone cannot separate those, and notably the human heard the prosody and overrode it with syntax ("felt that speaker wanted to speak more"), so a prosody-only model can beat a person on exactly that class. Next: round two of the error-listening kit (built, 32 clips of the most expensive out-of-fold mistakes), then a small from-scratch CNN over log-mel of the last 1.5 s with augmentation — the handcrafted plateau is a feature-vocabulary ceiling, not a model-capacity one (every classifier we tried lands at Hindi hard_auc 0.61–0.71).
